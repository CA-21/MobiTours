Democratic Republic of Congo's Historia

The history of Congo began to be truly recorded in the first contacts with Europeans and populations Arabs to the 14th century Ntinu Wene.

Historia
- 13th and 14th centuries: the discovery of Empires and the United well organized throughout the country, the best known are: Lunda, Luba, Kongo, Kuba, Zande ...
- 1482: Diego Cao, Portuguese navigator, discovered the mouth of the Congo River
- 1877-1884: Shipping Stanley and his deputies which Hanssens, Van de Velde Van Gele, Valcke Coquilhat.
- 1885: Creation, the Berlin Conference, the Congo Free State under the auspices of King Leopold II of Belgium.
- 1908: Creation of the Belgian Congo
- 1960: Independence of the Republic of Congo
- 1964: The country is called the Democratic Republic of Congo
- 1965: Military coup and accession of Lieutenant General Mobutu in power
- 1971: The country is called the Republic of Zaire
1997 - Accession of Laurent-Desire Kabila to power at the end of the War of Liberation of the AFDL. The country becomes the Democratic Republic of Congo
- 2001: Assassination, January 16, President Laurent-Desire Kabila. Major General Joseph Kabila became Head of State and sworn January 23, 2006
